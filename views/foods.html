FOODS


Discover the Philippines: A Land of Enchantment and Adventure. From the Pristine Beaches of Boracay to the Majestic Rice Terraces of Banaue, Each Destination Offers a Unique Experience. Immerse Yourself in the Warm Hospitality of the Filipino People. Let Your Journey Begin!



Lechon is a traditional Filipino dish made from a whole roasted pig, known for its crispy skin and tender meat. It's a popular choice for special occasions and celebrations.

Lumpia is a fried dish consisting of a thin wrapper filled with a mixture of ground meat, vegetables, and sometimes shrimp or other seafood.

Kare=Kare is a traditional Filipino stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables cooked in a rich peanut sauce. It is often served with fermented shrimp paste and rice.

Sinigang is a Filipino sour soup or stew characterized by its tangy flavor, which is achieved by using tamarind as a souring agent and is a popular comfort food.


Pancit is a Filipino noodle dish that's stir-fried with vegetables, meat, and/or seafood. It's a popular dish in the Philippines, often served during special occasions.

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made by marinating chicken or pork, in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices before simmering it until tender.

Sisig is a dish made from parts of pig's head and liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It's often served on a sizzling plate and is great with beer.

Kakanin is a type of rice cake made from glutinous rice and coconut milk, often flavored with sugar and coconut. It's a popular dessert or snack with various regional variations and flavors.
